Problem

Understanding the Market Challenge

Mindset was conceived as a consumer meditation app focused on habit formation in a market dominated by Calm ($2B valuation) and Headspace ($320M funding). We were a self-funded 4-person team with no marketing budget.

Market Saturation

Impossible to Compete on Content

Consumer meditation market dominated by well-funded incumbents.

  • Headspace: 70M+ downloads, celebrity partnerships, enterprise B2B expansion
  • Calm: 100M+ downloads, $150M annual revenue, massive content library
  • Marketing spend to break through was well beyond what we had
  • No path to differentiation without funding
Resource Constraints

4-Person Team, 3-Month Runway

Self-funded with limited research budget and a critical validation deadline.

  • 3-month runway insufficient for traditional validation cycle
  • Limited budget for user research
  • 4-person team couldn't compete on scale
  • Self-funded model unsustainable without traction
Unclear Differentiation

"Better Habit Formation" Wasn't Enough

Generic value proposition couldn't overcome switching costs from established apps.

  • Users already invested in competitors
  • Switching costs too high
  • No unique market position
  • Ten Percent Happier, InsightTimer also in the space

We couldn't win in consumer wellness without a marketing budget. The honest question was: where could we? Healthcare workforce wellness made sense — employers pay for it, so there's no individual switching cost, and the market was much less crowded than B2C meditation. It wasn't a glamorous pivot, but it was the right one.

Design System Advantage

Why the Pivot Took 3 Weeks Instead of 4 Months

From the start, Mindset was built on a token-based system — not because I knew we'd pivot, but because it was the right way to build. That decision turned out to matter a lot.

Token Architecture

Three-Tier Token Structure

  • Primitive tokens — base colors, spacing, typography
  • Semantic tokens — brand-primary, surface-secondary
  • Component tokens — button-bg-default, input-border-focus

This structure meant that a brand identity swap required changing only the primitive layer — all semantic and component tokens cascaded automatically.

The Solution

From Mindset to Aegis

Healthcare workforce wellness management — a B2B platform enabling health plan sponsors to track and manage employee wellness implementation across their network.

Core Product Features

Implementation Management Dashboard

  • Total implementations tracker (Complete · In Progress · Not Started)
  • Filter and Sort by geography, go-live date, business requirements
  • Multi-tenant: supports multiple health plan clients simultaneously
  • Compliance-ready reporting for healthcare requirements
Target Context

Enterprise Health Plans

  • Western Dental — West region, multi-location rollout
  • Greenbrier Union Premium Health Care Plan
  • Admin-focused workflows for plan sponsors
  • Employer-sponsored access removes individual barrier to entry

The thing that made this market actually work for us: the employer buys it, not the employee. That changes everything — there's no acquisition cost per user, no competing against someone's existing Calm subscription, no "why would I switch?" objection. The plan sponsor (an HR team or health plan) makes the decision and rolls it out to their people.
